MONITORING SYSTEM FOR KINETIC ENERGY HARVESTING

In this project the idea was to track a person's heart rate with a sensor connected to a microcontroller in the user's wrist. This microcontroller would send the data to the user's cell phone through a Bluetooth connection and would be stored in a local and remote database to be studied as records.
One of the biggest constraints of this project, was the energy consumption. As the device was meant to be a kind of "smartwatch", it's battery couldn't be so big. That's why we decided to use a piezoelectric material to harvest some energy out of the user's movement, and in this way, extend the battery lifecycle.
In addition to this, we decided to use the Bluetooth Low Energy technology, to save even more energy decreasing the electric consumption of the microcontroller.
